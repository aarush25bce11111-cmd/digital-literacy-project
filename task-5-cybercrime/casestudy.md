###### Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. It typically occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

###### 

###### In a common scenario, a college student receives an email that looks like it is from their university administration. The email claims there is an urgent issue with their student account and asks them to click a link to verify their details. Step-by-step, the process unfolds as follows:



* ###### The attacker sends a convincing email with official logos and language.
* ###### The student clicks the provided link.
* ###### They are redirected to a fake website resembling the university portal.
* ###### The student enters login credentials or personal information.
* ###### The attacker captures this data and uses it for unauthorized access or financial fraud.

###### 

###### College students are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms, may lack awareness about cybersecurity, and tend to respond quickly to urgent messages.

###### 

###### The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose access to their accounts, face financial loss, or have their personal data misused. In some cases, attackers use compromised accounts to target others, spreading the scam further. This highlights the importance of digital literacy, careful verification of emails, and avoiding clicking on suspicious links.
